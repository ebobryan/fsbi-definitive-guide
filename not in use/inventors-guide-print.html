Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to patent an invention?
Patent attorneys charge $20,000-$100,000. For Sale By Inventor starts at $2,499 (full-service) or $999 (DIY with Patents4Less).
How long does it take to get a patent?
Typically 18-36 months from filing to issuance, depending on the complexity and USPTO backlog.
Do I need a prototype before filing a patent?
No. You can file a patent application with just drawings and a detailed description. We offer virtual models instead of expensive physical prototypes.
What's the difference between provisional and non-provisional patent?
A provisional patent is a lower-cost, temporary filing that gives you "patent pending" status for 12 months. A non-provisional patent is the full application that leads to an issued patent.
Can I patent an idea?
No. You can only patent an invention‚Äîa specific, tangible implementation of an idea. The idea must be novel, non-obvious, and useful.
What's the success rate for inventors?
Most sources cite a 3-5% success rate for inventors who go it alone. Our VIP Method‚Ñ¢ has helped issue 3,000+ patents‚Äîa significantly higher success rate.
